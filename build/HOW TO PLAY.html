How to play Starseeds

This is the star which you occupy at the start of the game.

For historical reasons, it is named Earth.

This is the chevron.

You can use it to select Earth.

If you are wearing the headset:

The chevron is stuck to your head, and you move it by moving your head.

If you're playing on a monitor:

Use the WASD keys to move horizontally.

Shift moves you up, and Control moves you down.

You can also move the chevron with the mouse and mouse wheel.

Good job.

Now Earth is selected.

You can go to space using the space bar.

Hold down the Space key while Earth is selected.

Then, move the chevron to another star.

This selects the new star.

Instead of the space bar, you can also hold down the camera button or either of the volume buttons. These are all on the right side of the headset.

(If you're using the headset, the button can get stuck if you push it quickly. Tap it again to release it.)

Now, let go of the space bar (or camera button).

A small green dot is launched.

This is a starseed.

It weighs 200 kilograms
and travels at 30,000 km/s.
Travel time to this star is
roughly 114 years.

Whew! That was a long 114 years.

Now there is another green star,
and more stars have been revealed
in its neighbourhood.

You can select the new green star
with the chevron, too, just like Earth.

Try launching another green dot!

Each green star can launch starseeds
to any star within 4 parsecs.

If you're out of range, the line will turn red.

You have five minutes.

Try to get as far away from Earth as you can!

The farther you can get, the higher your score.

Have fun in space.

Note: The positions of the stars are taken
from a real stellar database, the HYG Star Database.

This is neat, but it also affects the gameplay:

There are fewer stars in the database
when you are farther away from Earth.

So, when you are far away from Earth,
it's harder to find stars that are in range,
and there are many dead ends.

Watch out!

This is the only thing to watch out for.
Everything else in space is fine.



If you do run into trouble in space, you can hit F4 to restart.

Other tricks and tips:

Hit Esc or close the window to quit.

Use arrow keys to move the whole starfield somewhere more convenient.

Use - and = keys to make the whole starfield smaller or bigger.
